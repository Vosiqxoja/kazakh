What Is Kazakh?

  • Kazakh (Qazaq tili / Қазақ тілі) is a Turkic language in the Kipchak branch.
  • It is the state language of Kazakhstan and has official status in Russia's Altai Republic.
  • It is also spoken by Kazakh communities in China, Mongolia, Uzbekistan, Russia, and beyond.
  • This presentation covers usage, speakers, status, schools, script, map, video, and a key Kazakh artist today.

Sources: Britannica, Wikipedia, 50languages

Where Is Kazakh Spoken?

  • Kazakh is spoken across all regions of Kazakhstan.
  • Large communities are found in Xinjiang (China) and Bayan-Olgii (Mongolia).
  • Smaller diaspora groups live in Russia, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, Turkey, Germany, and other countries.

Key labels to mention while presenting: Kazakhstan, Xinjiang, Bayan-Olgii.

How Many Speakers? Language Status

~15 to 16 million speakers worldwide

Around 13 million people in Kazakhstan reported speaking Kazakh in census-based reporting.

Safety scale: Safe -> Vulnerable -> Endangered

  • Not endangered overall.
  • Sometimes described as somewhat vulnerable in urban and higher-education domains.

Government Efforts: Protection and Gaps

Laws

Constitution and Language Law define Kazakh as the state language and protect language rights.

Promotion

National programs since 1991 increased Kazakh use in education, media, administration, and business.

Challenges

Russian remains strong in some ministries, major cities, and parts of higher education.

Sources: Law on Languages, Qazinform, OSCE Academy, TASS

Schools: Is Kazakh a Main Teaching Language?

  • Yes. Kazakhstan has thousands of schools where Kazakh is the main language of instruction.
  • Approximate school profile (2019): 51% Kazakh-medium, 17% Russian-medium, 31% bilingual.
  • Reported totals: 3,749 Kazakh-medium, 1,195 Russian-medium, 2,046 bilingual schools.

Kazakh Alphabet and Script

  • Historically, Kazakh has been written in Arabic, Latin, and Cyrillic scripts.
  • In Kazakhstan today, the main script is Cyrillic-based (42 letters).
  • Distinct Kazakh letters include ә, ғ, қ, ң, ө, ұ, ү, һ, і (shown in the chart).
  • A gradual transition toward a modern Latin alphabet is planned.

Main Country Map and Population

  • Kazakhstan is the main country where Kazakh is the state language and widely spoken.
  • Estimated population in early 2026: about 20.5 million.
  • Kazakhstan is the 9th-largest country by land area, with low population density.

Kazakh Artist Today: Dimash Qudaibergen

  • Dimash Qudaibergen (b. 1994) is a globally famed Kazakh singer, songwriter, and multi-instrumentalist known for his 6+ octave vocal range.
  • He blends classical (bel canto), pop, and traditional Kazakh music styles into his performances.
  • Gained international stardom after winning major contests and appearing on China's Singer 2017.
  • While singing in over a dozen languages, he actively tours global arenas sharing Kazakh folk songs (like Daididau) and culture worldwide.
